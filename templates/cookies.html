Cookie Policy

Transparency about how SecuTrace uses (or doesn't use) cookies and browser storage.

Last Updated: January 2025

The Short Version

TL;DR: SecuTrace does NOT use cookies. We only use localStorage for your search history, which stays entirely in your browser and is never sent to our servers.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites store on your device. They can be used for various purposes:

  • Session Cookies: Remember you're logged in
  • Preference Cookies: Store your settings
  • Analytics Cookies: Track your behavior
  • Advertising Cookies: Profile you for targeted ads

SecuTrace uses NONE of these.

What We Use Instead: localStorage

Instead of cookies, SecuTrace uses a browser feature called localStorage. Here's how it differs:

Feature Cookies localStorage
Sent to server with requests Yes No
Accessible by server Yes No
Can track across sites Yes (third-party) No
Stays on your device only No (sent with requests) Yes
You have full control Partial Yes

What We Store in localStorage

SecuTrace stores only one thing in localStorage:

secutrace_history - Your recent search queries (up to 20 items)

Data Structure:

  • indicator: The IP, domain, URL, or hash you searched
  • type: The type of indicator (IP, Domain, URL, Hash)
  • timestamp: When you performed the search

Purpose:

  • Allow you to quickly re-run recent lookups
  • Provide convenience without requiring an account
  • Keep your workflow efficient

How to Clear Your Data

You have complete control over the data stored in localStorage:

Option 1: Use Our Clear Button

On the main page, click the "Clear History" button below the history section.

Option 2: Browser Settings

Clear site data through your browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data → Cookies and site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy → Cookies and Site Data → Clear Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data

Option 3: Developer Tools

Press F12, go to Application/Storage tab, find localStorage, and delete the secutrace_history key.

Third-Party Cookies

SecuTrace does not include any third-party services that set cookies:

  • No Google Analytics
  • No Facebook Pixel
  • No advertising networks
  • No social media trackers
  • No embedded third-party content

The only external resources we load are Bootstrap CSS/JS from the official CDN for styling purposes.

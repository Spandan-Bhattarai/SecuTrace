Privacy Policy

Your privacy is our priority. We believe in transparency about how we handle your data.

Last Updated: January 2025

Our Privacy Commitment

TL;DR: SecuTrace does not collect, store, or track any personal data. Your searches are not logged on our servers. Period.

SecuTrace is designed with privacy at its core. We built this platform because we believe security researchers and professionals deserve tools that respect their privacy while helping them investigate threats.

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • Search Queries: We do not log or store any indicators (IPs, domains, URLs, hashes) you analyze
  • Personal Information: No accounts, no email collection, no sign-ups required
  • Browsing Behavior: No analytics, no tracking scripts, no behavioral profiling
  • Device Information: No fingerprinting, no device identification
  • Location Data: No IP logging, no geolocation tracking

Local Storage

SecuTrace uses your browser's localStorage to enhance your experience:

  • Search History: Your recent searches are stored locally in your browser only
  • Purpose: Allows you to quickly re-run previous lookups
  • Control: You can clear this history anytime using the "Clear History" button
  • Privacy: This data never leaves your browser and is not accessible to us

localStorage is not the same as cookies. It's a browser feature that stores data entirely on your device.

Third-Party Services

When you perform a lookup, SecuTrace queries third-party threat intelligence APIs on your behalf:

  • VirusTotal - File and URL analysis
  • AbuseIPDB - IP abuse reporting database
  • Shodan - Internet-connected device search
  • AlienVault OTX - Open Threat Exchange
  • IPInfo - IP geolocation and ASN data
  • URLhaus - Malware URL database
  • ThreatFox - IOC sharing platform
  • MalwareBazaar - Malware sample exchange

These services may have their own privacy policies. Your indicator data is sent to these APIs to retrieve threat intelligence. We recommend reviewing their respective privacy policies if you have concerns.

Server-Side Processing

When you submit a lookup:

  • Your indicator is processed in memory only
  • API requests are made to third-party services
  • Results are aggregated and returned to you
  • No data is written to disk or databases
  • No request logs containing your indicators are kept

Your Rights

Since we don't collect personal data, there's nothing to request, modify, or delete. However, you have full control over:

  • Browser Data: Clear localStorage through your browser settings or our "Clear History" button
  • Usage: Use SecuTrace anonymously without any account
  • Opt-out: Simply stop using the service - no data remains
